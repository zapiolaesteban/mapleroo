00 / Executive Summary

The shop window doesn't match the shelf.

Mapleroo sells a premium-priced product for serious endurance athletes — but the brand, packaging, and digital experience read as early-stage. The product is real. The story is real. The science is real. The presentation is what's holding it back.

⊕ Premium Gap
$95.99

Priced premium, packaged early-stage.

A 24-pack of gels retails at $95.99 — competitor sets sit between $55–$70. The price is making the premium claim. The packaging isn't backing it up.

⊕ Site Performance

PageSpeed scores collapsing.

Desktop and mobile performance below 46 in Google PageSpeed. SEO under 85. This directly affects rank, CPC, and bounce — every channel pays for it.

⊕ Story Gap

01 / Clarity Test

Three seconds. Five questions.

Open the homepage. Don't scroll. Can a stranger tell what this is, who it's for, and why they should care — before they decide to bounce? This is the test every FMCG brand pays for, in conversion or in lost traffic.

What is this product? Q 01

Clear it's a maple-based energy product.

Above the fold only one product is introduced. Different athletes have different fuel needs — the homepage should hint at the full range so the right buyer keeps scrolling.

Friction
Who is it for? Q 02

"Endurance athletes" — but which ones?

If the product also serves trail runners, cyclists, surf-life-saving, ultra crowds, that needs to be on the homepage. Showing real athletes from those sports converts better than abstract claims.

Friction
Why is it different? Q 03

Maple is the answer — buried below fold.

The maple-syrup story is the differentiator and it's strong. But the benefits should be the first thing a visitor sees. Right now they have to scroll to find the reason to care.

Below fold
Premium or budget? Q 04

Price says premium. Brand says startup.

A 24-pack of gels at $95.99 (pre-discount) sits well above competitors at $55–$70. The packaging and digital brand don't match that positioning. The mismatch creates friction at checkout.

Mismatch
Would I trust this brand with my money? Q 05

Not yet. The site doesn't close the sale.

Trust signals (reviews, athlete proof, science backing) are present somewhere — but easy to miss in homepage clutter. The brand doesn't visually justify the premium price point. For a first-time visitor weighing $95 against a known competitor, the deciding push isn't there.

02 / Packaging & Brand Hierarchy

The brand is the biggest gap.

The pack uses the space efficiently but doesn't earn the premium. The biggest single thing letting Mapleroo down is the brand identity itself — it doesn't reflect the quality of what's inside the pack, or the price on the shelf.

×
Retail-mature, or early-stage?

⊕ The single biggest finding

The biggest thing letting down the brand is the brand itself. Mapleroo is anchored to Australia in name and story — but the website, photography and pack design say nothing about Ironman, surf-life-saving, or Australian endurance culture. Lean into the name. Own the story. Show the athlete.

08 / Priority Fixes

Five moves. Most impact, least effort.

Ranked by leverage. Each item is judged on impact (how much it moves the score), effort (how much resource it takes), and timeline (how fast results compound). Do them in order.

Brand-up the visual identity.

The brand is the single biggest gap. Photography, packaging refinement, an Australian endurance visual language (Ironman, surf-coast, bunch rides). Lean into the "roo" — the name is doing nothing right now.

ImpactCritical
EffortHigh
Timeline8–12 wk

Fix the foundation.

PageSpeed under 46 is silently bleeding revenue across every channel. Lazy-load imagery, defer non-critical scripts, compress hero assets, audit theme-injected JS. This is non-negotiable before any paid spend.

ImpactHigh
EffortMedium
Timeline2–3 wk

Simplify the homepage.

Cut competing messages above the fold. One hero product OR one clear range pivot. Promote benefits ("low GI, electrolyte-rich, real food fuel") above pricing. Move trust signals into the hero, not into the footer.

ImpactHigh
EffortLow
Timeline1–2 wk

Switch on discovery.

A small AdWords spend defending brand + growing "energy gels" — the term Mapleroo is invisible on. Set up Google Shopping. Build a review-request flow into post-purchase email. Pitch one authority article per quarter.

ImpactMedium
EffortLow
Timeline2–4 wk

Re-edit product pages.

Reorder PDP hierarchy — benefit copy above pricing. Write sport-specific use cases ("for the marathon," "for the bunch ride," "for the recovery hour"). Restructure ingredient panel. Photograph each SKU consistently.

ImpactMedium
EffortMedium
Timeline3–4 wk
Where we go from here

The product is real. The brand needs to catch up.

Mapleroo has earned the premium price tag through formula, founder story, and category insight. The next 90 days are about making the brand presentation match the product reality. We'd recommend the brand-system rebuild and the homepage simplification first — they unlock everything else.
