02 / Origin

The collapse.
The promise.
The brand.

mapleROO didn't start with a whiteboard. It started with Eric Poulin lying in a medical tent after his second Ironman — fit, finished, and failed by the fuel he'd trusted.

The training had gone in. The fitness was there. But during the race, his stomach shut down. The gels he'd relied on all season stopped working. Discomfort became pain. Pain became crisis. He crossed the line and collapsed.

Lying in the medical tent, Eric made two promises to his wife Karine. First: no more Ironman. Then the Poulin family moved from Canada to Australia, where coastal mornings, sunrise cyclists and pre-dawn running clubs slowly gave him back the sport he loved.

The second promise: if he raced again, he'd fuel differently. Or he'd build it himself.

The answer had been growing in his home country all along. Certified organic maple syrup — tapped from Canadian forests, low glycemic, naturally electrolyte-rich, gentle on the gut that modern gels had punished. He combined it with just enough of what the body actually needs: clean carbohydrates, real salt, a careful measure of natural caffeine. Nothing synthetic. Nothing artificial. Nothing apologized for.

And so mapleROO was born — tapped in Canada, fueled in Australia, built by an athlete who refused to accept that performance had to mean chemistry.

03 / Coffee Thesis

Why the pack looks like
specialty coffee.

Every design decision is borrowed, deliberately, from the world of single-origin coffee. It's not an aesthetic — it's a truth. Coffee culture and endurance culture already share the same customer.

Coffee is already endurance fuel.

Caffeine is one of the only legal, research-proven ergogenic aids in endurance sport. Every cyclist's ride starts with an espresso. Every marathoner drinks coffee before the start line. Coffee culture and endurance culture already share the same customer — we're just the first gel brand to admit it, and our hero SKU is literally Maple · Coffee.

Coffee bags tell origin stories.

Single origin. Tasting notes. Producer credits. Specialty coffee treats the ingredient with reverence. Maple syrup has every bit as much provenance — tap location, harvest season, grade. We just give it the same respect. Craft isn't a price, it's a standard of attention.

04 / The Mark

The leaf we already had.

mapleROO has always had a distinctive maple leaf — nine points, a pointed triangular stem, a silhouette sharper and more confident than the Canadian flag leaf. We're not redesigning it. We're giving it the system it deserves.

THE mapleROO MARK

Nine points.
One direction.

The mark is a 9-point maple silhouette with an elongated triangular stem. That stem is the quiet genius of the logo — it's already a natural arrow, pointing downward, giving the mark inherent motion and hierarchy. The leaf was always pointing. We just had to recognize it.

Flat single colour. No gradients. No outlines. Production-ready at any scale, any surface, any ink. A printer's dream and a favicon's friend.

05 / The System

Light = caffeinated.
Dark = caffeine free.

The pack itself tells you what's inside. Cream-bodied packs with a terracotta top band signal the caffeinated variants — for race day, long rides, the hours that demand it. Espresso-bodied packs with flavour-toned bands signal caffeine free — for recovery sessions, afternoon training, the athlete who's already had their dose.

06 / Positioning

The strategic architecture.

What the brand stands for, where it plays, how it wins. One page. Keep it next to every design, every campaign, every pack iteration.

The Brand

mapleROO — natural sports fuel, born Canadian, raised Australian, fueled by maple and belief.

The Promise

Clean fuel for the long way round. Performance without the chemistry. Endurance without the bargain.

The Enemy

The synthetic-sweet, gut-wrecking, lab-engineered gel that treats the athlete as a carbohydrate delivery problem, not a human body.

The Target

The serious endurance athlete who has outgrown candy fuel. Cares about what's in the pack, where it's from, and how they'll feel tomorrow morning.

The Truth

Certified organic maple syrup, tapped from Canadian forests, is nature's endurance fuel. Low glycemic. Naturally electrolyte-rich. Gentle on the gut. Proven for a thousand years before a gel existed.

The Aesthetic

Patagonia Provisions × Blue Bottle Coffee. Warm paper tones, utilitarian typography, tasting-note language, generous white space. Every pack is a love letter to the ingredients.

The Difference

We don't win on fastest carbs per dollar. We win on how the athlete feels — during the race, after the race, and a decade from now when they're still racing.
